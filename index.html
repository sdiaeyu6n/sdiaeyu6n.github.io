  1. SEA: Evaluating Sketch Abstraction Efficiency via Element-level Commonsense Visual Question Answering
    J. Park, S. Choi, J. Seo, M. Sohn, Y. Kim, and J. Kim.
    CVPR 2026 (Accepted).
    View abstract & figure
    Representative figure to add (`images/pubs/sea.jpg`)

    A sketch is a distilled form of visual abstraction that conveys core concepts through simplified strokes while omitting extraneous detail. This paper introduces SEA, a reference-free metric for abstraction efficiency that evaluates semantic retention under visual economy, and presents CommonSketch, a semantically annotated sketch dataset for element-level evaluation.

  3. Exposing Blindspots: Cultural Bias Evaluation in Generative Image Models First Author
    H. Seo*, S. Choi*, M. Hong*, Y. Zhou, J. Kim, L. Ismaila, N. Etori, M. Agarwal, Z. Liu, J. Kim, and J. Oh.
    IASEAI 2026 Main Conference (Accepted).
    View abstract & figure
    Representative figure to add (`images/pubs/exposing_blindspots.jpg`)

    This work evaluates cultural bias in both text-to-image generation and image-to-image editing under a unified protocol across six countries and multiple cultural categories. The analysis shows country-agnostic defaults, cultural fidelity erosion under iterative edits, and superficial cue usage rather than context-aware cultural adaptation.

  4. Before We Trust Them: Decision-Making Failures in Navigation of Foundation Models
    J. Han*, J. Seo*, J. Min*, S. Choi, H. Seo, J. Kim, and J. Oh.
    AAAI 2026 Summer Symposium Series (Accepted).
    View abstract & figure
    Representative figure to add (`images/pubs/navigation_failures.jpg`)

    This paper investigates failure modes in foundation-model navigation reasoning across complete information, incomplete information, and safety-critical settings. Despite high aggregate success rates, models exhibit structural collapse, hallucinated reasoning, constraint violations, and unsafe decisions in critical cases.

  6. Evaluating Demographic Misrepresentation in Image-to-Image Portrait Editing
    H. Seo*, S. Choi*, M. Hong*, J. Kim, and J. Oh.
    View abstract & figure
    Representative figure to add (`images/pubs/demographic_misrepresentation.jpg`)

    This study analyzes demographic-conditioned failures in instruction-guided image-to-image portrait editing. It identifies soft erasure and stereotype replacement, and shows that identity preservation failures are uneven across demographics while prompt-level identity constraints can reduce drift for minority groups.

  7. Contour-Guided Query-Based Feature Fusion for Boundary-Aware and Generalizable Cardiac Ultrasound Segmentation
    Z. Ullah, S. Choi, and J. Kim.
    arXiv preprint, 2026 (Under Review).
    View abstract & figure
    Representative figure to add (`images/pubs/cgqr_net.jpg`)

    The paper proposes a contour-guided query refinement framework for cardiac ultrasound segmentation under low contrast, noise, and domain shift. By combining high-resolution features, contour-derived queries, and dual-head supervision, the method improves boundary precision and generalization across datasets.
