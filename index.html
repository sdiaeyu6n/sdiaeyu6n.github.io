Publications

  1. SEA: Evaluating Sketch Abstraction Efficiency via Element-level Commonsense Visual Question Answering
    J. Park, S. Choi, J. Seo, M. Sohn, Y. Kim, and J. Kim.
    CVPR 2026 (Accepted).
    View abstract & figure

    A sketch is a distilled form of visual abstraction that conveys core concepts through simplified yet purposeful strokes while omitting extraneous detail. Despite its expressive power, quantifying abstraction efficiency remains challenging. SEA introduces a reference-free metric that evaluates how economically a sketch preserves class-defining semantics, and CommonSketch provides 23,100 semantically annotated sketches across 300 classes for systematic element-level evaluation.

  2. Machine Learning Prediction of Common Bile Duct Stones Using Synthetic Data to Guide Emergency ERCP Decisions
    S. Kang*, N. Park*, I. S. Shin*, E. Lee, S. Choi, J. Jung, J. K. Lee, Y. N. Lee, J. H. Moon, K. R. Joo, J. S. Kim, and J. Kim.
    Scientific Reports, 2026.
    View abstract & figure

    This study develops and validates a machine learning model for common bile duct stone prediction using synthetic data augmentation. Across internal and external validation, the model achieved strong AUROC and substantially reduced unnecessary ERCP procedures while maintaining low false-negative rates, outperforming existing clinical guideline baselines.

  3. Exposing Blindspots: Cultural Bias Evaluation in Generative Image Models First Author
    H. Seo*, S. Choi*, M. Hong*, Y. Zhou, J. Kim, L. Ismaila, N. Etori, M. Agarwal, Z. Liu, J. Kim, and J. Oh.
    IASEAI 2026 Main Conference (Accepted).
    View abstract & figure

    Generative image models can produce strong visuals while still misrepresenting culture. This paper provides a unified benchmark across six countries and multiple categories to compare text-to-image generation and image-to-image editing. Results show Global-North defaults, fidelity erosion under iterative edits, and superficial stylistic cues instead of context-aware cultural edits.

  4. Before We Trust Them: Decision-Making Failures in Navigation of Foundation Models
    J. Han*, J. Seo*, J. Min*, S. Choi, H. Seo, J. Kim, and J. Oh.
    AAAI 2026 Summer Symposium Series (Accepted).
    View abstract & figure

    High success rates on navigation tasks do not necessarily imply reliable decision making by foundation models. Across six diagnostic tasks in three settings, the study finds structural failures, hallucinated reasoning, constraint violations, and unsafe decisions, demonstrating the need for failure-focused evaluation beyond aggregate scores.

  6. Evaluating Demographic Misrepresentation in Image-to-Image Portrait Editing
    H. Seo*, S. Choi*, M. Hong*, J. Kim, and J. Oh.
    View abstract & figure

    Demographic bias in text-to-image generation is well studied, but demographic-conditioned failures in instruction-guided image-to-image editing remain underexplored. This paper formalizes soft erasure and stereotype replacement, shows uneven identity preservation across demographics, and demonstrates that prompt-level identity constraints can reduce demographic drift without model retraining.

  7. Contour-Guided Query-Based Feature Fusion for Boundary-Aware and Generalizable Cardiac Ultrasound Segmentation
    Z. Ullah, S. Choi, and J. Kim.
    arXiv preprint, 2026 (Under Review).
    View abstract & figure

    Accurate cardiac ultrasound segmentation is difficult under low contrast, speckle noise, irregular boundaries, and domain shifts. CGQR-Net integrates multi-resolution features with contour-derived structural priors for boundary-aware refinement, improving segmentation accuracy, boundary precision, and cross-dataset robustness.

  8. StableSketcher: Enhancing Diffusion Model for Pixel-based Sketch Generation via Visual Question Answering Feedback
    J. Park, S. Choi, J. Seo, and J. Kim.
    arXiv preprint, 2025 (Under Review).
    View abstract & figure

    StableSketcher improves pixel-based hand-drawn sketch generation by fine-tuning the VAE for sketch-oriented latent decoding and introducing a VQA-based reinforcement signal for text-image alignment. The work also presents SketchDUO, a sketch-caption-QA dataset designed for instance-level training and evaluation.
